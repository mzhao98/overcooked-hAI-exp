We need your consent to proceed

You have been invited to take part in a research study: Collaborative Cooking with an AI Agent.

These collaborative cooking games are part of a research study conducted at Carnegie Mellon University. The purpose of the research is to understand human-AI teaming.

Procedures Your task is to perform 10 rounds of 1-minute cooking tasks with different AI partners. Instructions will be displayed for each task. You may periodically during and/or after the task be asked to respond to brief questions about your thoughts and feelings, including your preferences in collaborating with each agent.

Participant Requirements Participation in this study is limited to individuals age 18 and older who are fluent English speakers.

Risks The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during other online activities. These could include boredom or fatigue, associated with memorization tasks.

Benefits There may be no personal benefit from your participation in the study but the knowledge received may be of value to humanity.

Compensation & Costs The study is expected to last 10-20 minutes and you will be compensated $XXX.XX for your time. There will be no cost to you if you participate in this study.

Confidentiality By participating in this research, you understand and agree that Carnegie Mellon may be required to disclose your consent form, data and other personally identifiable information as required by law, regulation, subpoena or court order. Moreover, all information obtained in this study is strictly confidential. The US Department of Defense, the sponsor of this study, will have access to research records as part of their responsibilities for human subjects protection oversight of the study. The results of this research study may be used in reports, presentations, and publications, but the researchers will not identify you.

Otherwise, your confidentiality will be maintained in the following manner: Your data and online consent form will be kept separate. Your consent response will be stored in a secure virtually on Carnegie Mellon property and will not be disclosed to third parties. Sharing of data with other researchers will only be done in such a manner that you will not be personally identified. By participating, you understand and agree that the data and information gathered during this study may be used by Carnegie Mellon and published and/or disclosed by Carnegie Mellon to others outside of Carnegie Mellon. However, your name and other direct personal identifiers will not be mentioned in any such publication or dissemination of the research data and/or results by Carnegie Mellon. Note that per regulation all research data must be kept for a minimum of 3 years.
