We need your consent to proceed

You have been invited to take part in a research study: Collaborative Cooking with an AI Agent.

These collaborative cooking games are part of a research study conducted at Carnegie Mellon University. The purpose of the research is to understand human-AI teaming.

Procedures Your task is to perform 10 rounds of 1-minute cooking tasks with different AI partners. Instructions will be displayed for each task. You may periodically during and/or after the task be asked to respond to brief questions about your thoughts and feelings, including your preferences in collaborating with each agent.

Participant Requirements Participation in this study is limited to individuals age 18 and older who are fluent English speakers.

Risks The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during other online activities. These could include boredom or fatigue, or may include mild negative emotions when being reminded of certain life experiences. We expect these to be mild and will only last for a brief amount of time. There is also a small risk of breach of confidentiality by associating your data with your name; however, this risk is small since names are not stored in our data files. While in many studies of human behavior, including this one, we cannot tell you about everything we are examining since we don't want you to change your behavior, we would never mislead you about what data are being collected or about any anticipated risks.

Benefits There may be no personal benefit from your participation in the study but the knowledge received may be of value to humanity.

Compensation & Costs The study is expected to last 20-25 minutes and you will be compensated $4.00 (rate of $9.50 per hour) for your time. In addition, up to $5.00 bonus compensation depends on your performance and the performance of other members of your group. Thus, your total payment will range between [$4.00-$9.00]. There will be no cost to you if you participate in this study.

Confidentiality By participating in this research, you understand and agree that Carnegie Mellon may be required to disclose your consent form, data and other personally identifiable information as required by law, regulation, subpoena or court order. Moreover, all information obtained in this study is strictly confidential. The US Department of Defense, the sponsor of this study, will have access to research records as part of their responsibilities for human subjects protection oversight of the study. The results of this research study may be used in reports, presentations, and publications, but the researchers will not identify you.

Otherwise, your confidentiality will be maintained in the following manner: Your data and consent form will be kept separate. Your consent form will be stored on secure servers controlled by Carnegie Mellon and will not be disclosed to third parties. Sharing of data with other researchers will only be done in such a manner that you will not be personally identified. By participating, you understand and agree that the data and information gathered during this study may be used by Carnegie Mellon and published and/or disclosed by Carnegie Mellon to others outside of Carnegie Mellon. However, your name and other direct personal identifiers will not be mentioned in any such publication or dissemination of the research data and/or results by Carnegie Mellon. Note that per regulation all research data must be kept for a minimum of 3 years.
